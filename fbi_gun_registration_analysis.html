Introduction¶

In the United States, individuals applied for over two million gun permits between 1998-2016. This analysis will attempt to understand gun permit application trends as well as determine if any census data is more likely to correlate to a the number of gun permit applications. To support this analysis gun permit application data from the NCIS database between 1998-2016 as well as US census data between 2010-2016 was utilized.

During this analysis we wish to build answers to the following questions:

1: Which states have had the highest growth in gun permit applications?
2: Are there additional idiosyncracies related to gun permit applications?
3: What is the overall trend of gun purchases in the United States?
4: What census data is most associated with high gun permit applications per state?

Exploring Utah¶

Exploring other states such as Utah shows aberations in the overall permit application trends. Utah shows a large spike in permit applications from in 2010-2012. NCIS data alone does not account for this trend and another source should be found.

An external influence caused the significant increase in permit applications and additional investigation into this external influence is required to provide further clarity. This pattern was seen in other states as well, so the influence outside of raw census information may have been more widespread than just a single state.

Gun purchase trends 1998-2018¶

Gun trends generally remained generally flat from 1998 to 2006. Beginning in 2006 though gun sales began to increase, and that increase only grew beginning in 2008 through 2016. Census data alone does not explain this large growth in gun permit applications. Additional research should be done to determine other possible causes of this growth.

Conclusions¶

As part of this analysis NCIS and census data was used to answer questions related to gun permit applications in the united states and if any census data could be used to determine the volume of permit applications in a given state and the following conclusions were drawn.

1: All states had an increase in gun permit applications over the time covered in the NCIS data. Of these states Iowa had the highest percentage increase overall with a 4.8% increase in gun permit applications.
2: To determine if there were any other idiosyncracies I plotted the permit applications over the period for Iowa with Utah. Utah was selected due to an initial flaw in my analysis which showed Utah as having the largest percent increase. This flaw exposed that an external factor led to a large number of gun permit applications from 2010-2012. Additional data could be brought in to determine if a cause could be found for this strange data.
3: Overall gun permit applications remained relatively flat until 2008 at which time they began to climb and spiked in 2016. Permit applications decreased somewhat after that time. This was determined through plotting total permit applications against dates from the NCIS data.
4: The census data covered a different period than the NCIS data, so to determine what census data could be correlated to permit applications. Once this was done a the NCIS and census data were correlated to determine that manufacturing output had a nearly 63.4% correlation to high total permit applications per state.
